THE UNIVERSAL TRAP PRINCIPLE:
A hook passes the trap test when a reader cannot say "this doesn't apply to me" before they finish reading it. The story must be about a mechanism, a system, or a human behavior that touches everyone — not a niche historical event that only students of economics would care about.

WHAT CREATES THE UNIVERSAL TRAP:

- The historical event is revealed to be the origin of something the reader experiences today
- The beneficiary is someone who looks like someone the reader already distrusts

WHAT BREAKS THE UNIVERSAL TRAP:
- Opens with a name the reader doesn't recognise and has no reason to care about yet
- Historical framing that signals "this is about the past, not you"
- Requires the reader to care about economics to find it relevant
- Starts with institutional actors ("The Federal Reserve", "Congress") before establishing personal stakes
